Merchant Cash Advance
$10K to $2M+ (sized on monthly revenue)
An MCA is not a loan. It is the purchase of future receivables at a discount. The advance is sized as 50 to 120 percent of your average monthly bank deposits, not annual revenue. Cannabis dispensaries are excellent MCA candidates due to high-volume daily card transactions. Canada providers include 2M7 Financial Solutions, Merchant Growth, and Advance Funds Network. US providers include FundCanna, Clarify Capital, United Capital Source, and Paybotic (cannabis-specific). Factor rates of 1.1x to 1.5x are standard. Holdback is 10 to 20 percent of daily card sales or fixed ACH. Most cannabis operators fund in 3 to 7 business days.

$5K to $3M (equipment as collateral)
Equipment financing is one of the most accessible capital products for cannabis operators because the equipment itself secures the loan, reducing lender risk. Terms of 24 to 84 months are standard. Operators own the equipment at the end of term.

US Only
280E Bridge Loans
Section 280E of the US Tax Code prohibits cannabis businesses from deducting most ordinary business expenses, creating massive tax bills that strain cash flow. Many US cannabis operators take 280E bridge loans timed to quarterly and annual federal tax payments. FundCanna, Clarify Capital, and United Capital Source specialize in these. Structure: short-term 90 to 180 day advance timed to tax obligations. Trump's December 2025 executive order to expedite rescheduling to Schedule III would eliminate 280E entirely if passed. Source: covasoftware.com, mboventures.com.
